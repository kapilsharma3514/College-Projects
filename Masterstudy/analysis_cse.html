Dear GATE CS Aspirants,

GATE (Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering) is an online examination conducted across the country to test the candidates in various undergraduate subjects of Engineering, Technology or Architecture, and post graduate subjects of Science. GATE exam is a gateway for the students to secure admission in post graduate programs like M.Tech, M.E, M.S, direct PhD, etc. in higher education institutions (IITs, IISc, NITs, IIITs, etc.) that are centrally funded.

The GATE exam is jointly conducted by the seven IITs (Delhi, Bombay, Kharagpur, Kanpur, Guwahati, Roorkee and Madras) and IISc (Indian Institute of Science). The responsibility for conduct and coordination of the GATE 2016 Exam has been given to IISc. GATE examination is scheduled to be conducted between 30th January, 2016 and 7thFebruary, 2016.

The GATE Exam is a single paper online examination of 3 hours, with 65 questions carrying a total of 100 marks. There are 55 Questions which are covered from technical and mathematics and remaining 10 Questions are from aptitude. Both Aptitude and Technical sections has 1 mark and 2 mark objective type / numerical type questions.


Negative marking and extensive competition in GATE Exam make it one of the toughest examinations in India for postgraduate admissions in IITs or IISc. There is no limit in the number of attempts in GATE and failing in GATE attempt may waste year(s) in your life. Thus, the preparation stage is very crucial for the examination.

In previous GATE Computer science Papers, around 30 percentage of GATE marks are covered only for the questions of numerical type. So, minimum 30 marks would be expected for numerical based type questions in the GATE Exam 2016.

Subject wise weightage for GATE 2015 Computer Science Paper:

Mathematics, Aptitude, Algorithms, Programming and Data structures are having the weightage more than 50 marks in GATE Exam. So I would suggest you to prepare these subjects with top priority for cracking the GATE.

GATE 2015 & 2014 Questions were covered from the following important topics:

  • Data Structure and Algorithms: Programming, Recursion, Algorithm analysis, Heap trees, Sorting, Graphs, and Hashing.
  • Operating Systems: CPU scheduling, Deadlocks, memory management, virtual memory, and Disk management.
  • Engineering Mathematics: Graphs, functions, relations, mathematical logic, Linear Algebra, and Calculus
  • Digital: Minimization, combinational circuits, and sequential circuits
  • Computer Organization: Pipelining, and Cache organization.
  • Databases: SQL and ER-diagram
  • Computer Network: Data Link Layer, TCP, Congestion control, and IP addressing.
  • Theory of Computation: Finite Automata, and Context free languages
  • Compiler Design: Parsing

Understanding the above topics will help you to crack the GATE 2016. These topics are frequently asked in the GATE. Hence it helps you to score top marks in GATE. Follow the key points to understand the GATE pattern with detailed analysis which are explained below.

Key points of GATE Computer Science Paper:

  • Answering Numerical Type Questions will not carry the negative marking.
  • Aptitude section in GATE CS Exam paper is easier to get good marks in GATE.
  • In technical section, 1 mark questions can be answered quickly and do not waste your time in answering the 1 mark questions. You must spend sufficient time to solve 2 Mark questions..
  • Easy questions are possible from Aptitude, mathematics, programming, data structure, compiler design, digital logic, and database.
  • Difficult questions are possible from operating system, computer organization, computer networks, algorithms and theory of computation.
  • In computer science, many questions are conceptual based. So recall the definitions and concepts required for each question as quick as possible in actual GATE Exam 2016.

NOTE:

  • Do not assume anything before exam to attempt the GATE paper.
  • Every question is equally important in GATE exam.
  • You cannot decide any question is easy or hard without reading the question completely.
  • Understand every question and then decide whether you can answer it or not.
  • Do not decide the level of paper while answering the GATE Exam.
